= EXPLAINME — core-network-outpost, in plain terms
:toc: preamble

*What is this?* A small, low-power box that quietly makes your home network faster,
safer, and more private — and that you can actually *understand, rebuild, and repair*.

== The problem it solves

Your home network runs on a box your ISP handed you: you don't control it, can't
inspect it, and can't fix it. So when the line stutters under load (a video call
breaks up while something uploads), or every device is fed ads and trackers, or you
want a stable name and your own web presence — you're stuck with what you were given.

Commercial "network security boxes" (eBlocker, Bitdefender BOX, ...) tried to help,
then *shut down* and left their buyers stranded. A box you can't rebuild is a box
that can be taken away from you.

== What it does

* *Blocks ads, trackers, and malware domains* for every device (AdGuard Home).
* *Kills bufferbloat* — keeps ping and jitter low even when the line is busy (CAKE
  smart-queue shaping), so calls and games stay smooth.
* *Keeps accurate, authenticated time* (chrony) — quietly load-bearing for security.
* *A firewall that says "no" by default*, and can go *dark* (services invisible until
  you knock).
* *A stable name* for your connection (dynamic DNS), and — optionally — *your own
  independent web page* and properly-authenticated email DNS.

== Why it's built the way it is

. *Dependability first.* If security isn't dependable it's dangerous; if the box
  isn't dependable you'll ditch it and lose everything it gave you. So it is boring,
  reproducible, and self-healing *on purpose*.
. *Never a juggernaut.* It splits into two single-minded boxes — a boring, always-on
  *Core* that just works, and an optional, exciting *Frontier* for experimental bits —
  so one failure or breach can't take the whole thing down.
. *You're not alone.* It's one layer among many (your router, your devices, Cloudflare,
  the community), so it stays light and doesn't try to do everything.
. *Reproducible + yours.* Everything is config-as-code in git, pinned and verifiable.
  No vendor to shut down; rebuild on new hardware in an afternoon.
. *Efficient + accessible.* Runs on ~10 W and reuses hardware you already own — speed
  and security without a power-hungry box or a top-end chip.

== Who it's for

* *Anyone* wanting a faster, cleaner, more private home network — start with the *Core*
  on almost any spare device.
* *IndieWebbers* who want to own their web presence — see the wiki's IndieWeb track.
* *Developers / platform maintainers* who want the whole estate — the *Frontier* and
  its modules; see the maintainer track.
